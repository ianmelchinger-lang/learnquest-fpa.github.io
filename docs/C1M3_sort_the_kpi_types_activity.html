KPI thinking and selection

Sort a reporting pack into inputs, diagnostics, and outputs

Handed a reporting pack with dozens of figures, the hardest question isn't which numbers are correct. It's which ones actually matter. A KPI earns that name only once it has been sorted by type, so that a business leader can trace a disappointing result back down to the driver behind it. Sort seven real figures below, then trace one output back to its cause.

FP&A stands for financial planning and analysis. A metric is any number you can measure; a KPI is a metric someone has deliberately classified and tied to a decision. The classification test sorts every candidate into one of three types. A few terms before you start.

Output KPI
An achieved result the business is accountable for — the thing everything else is meant to explain.
Diagnostic KPI
A measure of the efficiency or quality of the process connecting an input to an output.
Input KPI
A resource or activity level that drives the process — something a team commits or moves directly.
KPI hierarchy
Inputs, diagnostics, and outputs arranged so a result at the top can be traced downward to the driver most likely responsible.
