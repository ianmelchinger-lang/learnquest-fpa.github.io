Reading a P&L

Find the line that moved most against budget

Every month a business compares what actually happened with the plan it set in advance, the budget. The gap on each line is the variance, and it points to where the month went off plan. Your job here is to read a short profit-and-loss statement and find the one line with the biggest gap.

A profit-and-loss statement (also called an income statement) lists what a business earned and what it spent to earn it. Below are the four line items on this month's statement, in the order you read them, with the actual result beside the budget plan. The variance is left for you to work out.

Revenue
Money earned from customers this month.
COGS
Cost of goods sold, the direct cost of delivering the service that earned the revenue.
Loss provision
Money set aside to cover expected losses, such as fraud or defaults.
Operating expense
The running costs of the business, such as salaries, systems and overhead, not tied to a single transaction.
